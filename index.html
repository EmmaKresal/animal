| Elephant Biology |

Elephant, is the largest living land animal, characterized by its long trunk (elongated upper lip and nose), columnar legs, and huge head with temporal glands and wide, flat ears. Elephants are grayish to brown in colour, and their body hair is sparse and coarse. The trunk, also called a proboscis, and is used for breathing, bringing food and water to the mouth, and grasping objects. Tusks, which are derived from the incisor teeth, serve both as weapons and as tools for moving objects

| Elephant Ecology |


Elephants roaming in the African Savanna.

They are found most often in savannas, grasslands, and forests but occupy a wide range of habitats, including deserts, swamps, and highlands in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. They are very adaptable to their surrounds. African Elephants clear away trees and plants so that new forms of vegetation can grow there and they form emigrational paths that they continue to follow year after year. This allows them to take advantage of the foods that grow in various areas.
